ABOUT

A History of Market Win

Timeline

1960, Start-up

Fresh out of college, Mary, our Queen of Market Win, hits upon the idea of promoting the lesser known local businesses to the wider community. Setting up shop in the spare bedroom of her parents' end terrace, she puts her machinations to paper and writes a business plan.

1961, Our First Client

After months of relentless phone calls, much to the dismay of her ever-loving parents, and plenty of door-knocking, Mary lands her first client - Mr. Jones the greengrocer, who agrees to pay her one bob a week, including fees, to advertise his most freshest of fresh cucumbers, among other vegetarian delights, however she sees fit.

1963, Office Space

With Mary's canny approach and an eye for a good deal, word of Mr. Jones's booming trade soon spreads to the other business owners on Corporation Street. Shortly after, Mary has all the even-numbered shops signed up to her agency and can afford to rent her own premises, allowing her mother the space to finally create her own knitting room.

1964, Minions

All the odd-numbered businesses on Corporation Street take note of the shiny new marketing agency in their midsts and sign up pretty sharpish. With more work than she can handle, Mary employs her first staff member, Joseph. He is immediately made 'head of even-numbers'.

1966, Recognition

The local council are overwhelmed by the increased tax revenue thanks to the roaring trade on Commercial Street. Mary is presented with the inaugural "Awesome Person Award'. In the process, she strikes a deal to manage the council's PR - the newly opened nuclear power plant has not gone down well with the NIMBY brigade!

1970, Stability

Mutually assured destruction averted, Joseph can't escape the fact that Mary really is such an awesome person. With Market Win's clients spread far and wide, and a team of established professionals in place to manage the odd and even numbered work, Joseph seizes the opportunity to propose. Being a serial winner, of course, Mary accepts.

1975, Expansion

Kids popped, and Joseph on house-husbandry detail, Mary set her sights on Europe and the opportunities available thanks to the UK's recent membership of the EU. Over the next three years, Mary's air travel is such, she has a noticeable, positive impact on share prices in the aviation sector. On February first 1979, Mary is invited to ring the opening bell on Wall Street as a thank you.

1985, Inventing the Yuppie

After cornering the North American market, Mary returns home triumphant. She isn't the only one riding high. After much champagne debauchery, all thanks to Mary's hard work, a leading firm of market traders turn to her to rebrand their flailing image. Thus, the 'Yuppie' is born.

2000s, Domination

Having foreseen the possibilities of the World Wide Web, Mary spent the 1990s learning to code. It is on these very pages that you will find the fruit of her labours and the front-end of a global empire greater than the combined wealth of all the FAANGs put together.
